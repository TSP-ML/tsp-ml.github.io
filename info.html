Understanding the Travelling Salesman Problem

Discover one of computer science's most famous optimization challenges and how TSP(ML) makes it accessible to everyone.

What is the Travelling Salesman Problem?

The Travelling Salesman Problem (TSP) is a classic optimization problem that asks: "Given a list of cities and the distances between each pair of cities, what is the shortest possible route that visits each city exactly once and returns to the origin city?"

While this might sound simple, the TSP is actually one of the most intensively studied problems in computational mathematics. As the number of cities increases, the number of possible routes grows factorially, making it computationally challenging to find the optimal solution.

The Complexity Challenge

5 cities 12 possible routes
10 cities 181,440 possible routes
20 cities 60+ quintillion routes

Why is TSP Important?

The applications of TSP solutions extend far beyond theoretical computer science

Logistics & Delivery

Optimize delivery routes to save fuel, reduce costs, and improve customer satisfaction. Companies like UPS and FedEx use TSP algorithms to plan efficient delivery schedules.

Circuit Board Manufacturing

Minimize the drilling time in printed circuit board production by finding the optimal path for drilling holes, significantly reducing manufacturing costs.

DNA Sequencing

Arrange DNA fragments in the correct order by finding the shortest superstring that contains all fragments, crucial for genetic research and medicine.

Tourist Planning

Help travelers create efficient sightseeing routes that maximize their time and minimize travel between attractions in unfamiliar cities.

Satellite Scheduling

Optimize the order in which satellites capture images of different locations on Earth to minimize energy consumption and maximize coverage.

Manufacturing

Optimize machine scheduling and production line sequencing to minimize downtime and maximize efficiency in manufacturing processes.
